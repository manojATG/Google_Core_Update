Before You Leave Your Resort

When to leave — by resort area

The single most important Road to Hana decision is when you leave. Arriving too late means parking gridlock, sold-out banana bread, and fighting for a spot at every waterfall.

West Maui — Lahaina, Ka'anapali, Kā'anapali

Leave by 6:45 AM

Longest drive to Kahului before you even reach the road. Leave before 7 AM or you'll be stuck behind early risers who beat you to the first waterfall by an hour.

→ Drive time to Twin Falls: ~75 min

South Maui — Kihei, Wailea, Makena

Leave by 7:15 AM

Slightly shorter drive to Paia than West Maui. Leave no later than 7:15 — parking at Twin Falls fills by 9 AM on a busy day.

→ Drive time to Twin Falls: ~55 min

Central Maui — Kahului, Wailuku

Leave by 7:30 AM

Closest to the road start. Use this time advantage — stop in Paia to fuel up, grab food, and download the app before signal drops.

→ Drive time to Twin Falls: ~35 min

North Shore — Paia, Haiku

Leave by 8:00 AM

You're already at the starting line. Use the extra time to fuel up in Paia (last gas until Hana), stock food, and download the tour.

→ You're already at Twin Falls in 10 min
Pre-Trip Checklist

Do this before you leave your resort

Most people who have a bad Road to Hana day skipped one of these.

1

Book Wai'ānapanapa State Park

Mandatory reservation — no same-day option. Book at gostateparks.hawaii.gov up to 30 days in advance. Download your voucher to your phone before leaving — no signal at the park.

⚠ No voucher = no entry. Cars outside are towed.

2

Download the GuideAlong app + your tour

Do this at your resort on WiFi. Cell service drops past Paia and won't return reliably until you're near Hana. The app uses GPS only once downloaded — no data connection needed.

3

Fill up fuel in Kahului or Paia

No reliable gas station for 50+ miles after Paia. Fill the tank before you leave — do not assume you'll find a station.

4

Pack food for the whole day

The road has roadside stands (mostly cash only) but no sit-down restaurants until Hana town. Pack lunch and snacks in Paia. The Nahiku Marketplace is the only notable food stop mid-route — cash only.

5

Pack water shoes, not just sandals

Several waterfall pools have volcanic rock approaches that are slippery. Water shoes or reef shoes make a real difference. Also bring: sunscreen, insect repellant, a light rain layer, and toilet paper for the less-serviced stops.

The Piilani Highway Warning

Do not take Hwy 31 back. This is not a "rough road" advisory.

All major car rental companies explicitly prohibit the Piilani Highway in their rental agreements. If you drive it and get a flat tire or break down, your roadside assistance is void. You could wait hours with no cell signal in a remote area. The scenery on the south coast is beautiful — but it is not worth the risk.

Drive back the way you came. Always.

An 80-foot waterfall visible directly from the road — no hiking needed. One of the most dramatic roadside moments on the entire route. Audio gives it the history context it deserves.

Fuel, food, and restrooms. Turn around here to drive back safely. The Pipiwai Trail to Oheo Gulch (Seven Sacred Pools) is nearby if you're staying overnight.

Wai'ānapanapa State Park · Sample narration

The legend of the black sand

The black sand beneath your feet isn't sand at all — it's pulverised basalt, ground down by centuries of Pacific surf against the volcanic shoreline. Wai'ānapanapa in Hawaiian means "glistening fresh water," a name that comes from the freshwater caves just around this headland. Ancient Hawaiians believed these caves sheltered the spirit of a princess who fled here from a jealous king. Each April, tiny red shrimp bloom in the water — and the old legends say the red is her blood, still rising after all these centuries. The caves and the shrimp are real. The princess — you'll have to decide for yourself.

Common Questions

What people ask before they drive

Do I need a reservation for Black Sand Beach?

Yes — mandatory since 2021, no exceptions. Book at gostateparks.hawaii.gov up to 30 days ahead. Download your voucher before leaving Paia — there is no cell signal at Wai'ānapanapa State Park. Do not assume roadside or overflow parking outside the entrance is allowed; enforcement and towing are possible.

Can I drive the Piilani Highway back?

Technically possible in some conditions, but many rental agreements restrict remote or unpaved portions of Hwy 31. If you break down, roadside assistance may be limited and cell signal may be unavailable. Unless your rental agreement explicitly allows the route and you are comfortable with the risk, drive back via Hana Highway the way you came.

How long does the Road to Hana take?

Plan for a full day — 8 to 10 hours including stops. The drive alone is 2.5 hours each way. With Twin Falls, Black Sand Beach, the waterfall stops, and food, a well-paced day means arriving home by 5–6 PM if you leave before 7:30 AM.

Does GuideAlong work without cell service?

Yes — completely. Download the tour on WiFi before you leave. After that the app uses your phone's built-in GPS (which doesn't need data) to track your location and trigger stories. You will have no signal for most of the drive and won't need any.

What's the difference between this tour and the basic Road to Hana route?

This "All Routes" version includes 140+ audio points covering the main Hana Highway plus the Haleakalā Crater route, the Paia town introduction, and coastal route content. The standard tour covers only the main Hana Highway drive.

One purchase — how many people does it cover?

One purchase covers one car. Everyone in the vehicle hears the tour through your car speakers (Bluetooth or aux). You do not need one per person.
