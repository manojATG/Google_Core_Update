Before You Leave

Your Road to Hana departure checklist

Do all of this before you leave your resort. The road itself has limited services and no cell signal for long stretches.

01

Leave by 7:00–7:30 AM from your resort

West Maui (Lahaina area): leave by 7:00 AM. South Maui (Kihei, Wailea): 7:30 AM. Any later and you'll hit gridlock at Twin Falls and be competing for parking at every major stop. The road is narrow — early start means you drive with traffic, not against it.

02

Book Wai'ānapanapa State Park (Black Sand Beach) in advance

Reservations are mandatory and open 30 days ahead at midnight HST. Same-day booking is not available. Book at gostateparks.hawaii.gov. Download your voucher to your phone before you leave — there is no cell signal at the park.

⚠ Vehicles parked outside the park will be towed. No exceptions.

03

Fill up fuel in Kahului or Paia

There is no reliable gas station between Paia and Hana town — a stretch of roughly 50 miles. Fill your tank before leaving Paia. Do not assume you can top up along the way.

04

Download the tour app before Paia — cell service drops here

Once you pass Paia, you will not have reliable cell service for most of the drive. Download the Action Tour Guide app and your Road to Hana tour while you still have WiFi at your resort or in Paia. The tour works completely offline once downloaded.

05

Pack food — the road has very few meal options

Stock up in Paia before departing. There are roadside food trucks and stands but no restaurants until Hana town. Nahiku Marketplace (cash only) and Halfway to Hana are the main food stops. Do not count on finding a meal when you're hungry — pack enough for the whole day.

06

Pack water shoes, not just walking shoes

Several of the best waterfall stops require walking on slippery volcanic rock and sometimes wading through shallow streams. Standard hiking shoes work but water shoes or reef shoes are genuinely better. Sunscreen, insect repellant, and a light rain layer round out the essentials — the road passes through rainforest that earns its name.

What You'll Experience

Key stops along the route

The audio tour guides you to all of these automatically. You don't need to navigate — just drive and listen.

1
Free · No reservation

Twin Falls — Mile 2 from Paia

The first major stop and the most crowded. Arrive before 8:30 AM to walk to the falls without crowds. Short trail, good for all fitness levels. The audio tells you which trail junction leads to the better upper falls — most people miss it.

Field note: The lower falls are photographed but the upper falls are the ones worth seeing. Follow the audio's directions at the fork — they'll tell you exactly when you're there.
2
Free stop

Waikamoi Ridge Trail — the rainforest walk

A short loop trail through bamboo and eucalyptus that most drivers skip because they don't know it's there. 20–30 minutes. Completely different feel from the roadside stops — genuinely quiet and lush. Audio cues you when to pull over.

3
Free · Cash for food

Halfway to Hana stand — banana bread stop

This is the banana bread everyone talks about. Small roadside stand, cash only. Get here before 10 AM or it sells out. The audio warns you when you're approaching so you don't miss the turnout.

Field note: The banana bread is genuinely worth the stop. One loaf per car is the unwritten rule when it's busy.
4
Free viewpoint

Rainbow Eucalyptus Grove — Ke'anae

The trees with the paint-peeled multicolour trunks. Pull over for photos but don't block the road — there is a small designated area. The audio explains why the bark looks this way (it's genuinely fascinating and not what most people assume).

5
Free · Restroom available

Ke'anae Peninsula — historic lava field and church

A lava field that looks like the moon, a tiny fishing village, and a stone church that survived everything Maui could throw at it. This is one of the audio tour's most detailed stops — the story of this community is one of the route's best.

6
⚠ Reservation required

Wai'ānapanapa State Park — Black Sand Beach

The most photographed stop on the road. Volcanic black sand, blowholes, sea caves, and a coastal trail. You must have a reservation booked in advance at gostateparks.hawaii.gov — no same-day booking, no walk-ins. Without a reservation you will be turned away at the gate. Budget 1–2 hours here.

Field note: Download your reservation voucher to your phone before leaving Paia. No cell signal here means you cannot access your email or booking confirmation at the park.
7
Most scenic

Wailua Falls — roadside viewpoint

A 80-foot waterfall you can see directly from the road. No hiking required. One of the most dramatic roadside moments on the entire route — the audio gives it the context it deserves.

8
Hana town · Full services

Hana Town — the end point (and turnaround)

Fuel, food, restrooms. If you're driving back the same day (the safe choice), turn around here. The Piilani Highway (Hwy 31) back through the south coast is scenic but prohibited by all major rental car companies — a breakdown or flat tire there means hours of waiting with no signal and no help.

⚠ Do not take the Piilani Highway unless you have a 4WD vehicle and have confirmed explicitly with your rental company. This is not a "rough road" warning — it is an insurance void and a potential rescue call.

The black sand beneath your feet isn't sand at all — it's pulverised basalt lava, ground down by centuries of Pacific surf crashing against this shoreline. Wai'ānapanapa in Hawaiian means "glistening fresh water," a reference to the freshwater caves just around the headland. Ancient Hawaiians believed these caves were home to the spirit of a princess who fled here to escape a jealous king. Every April, tiny red shrimp bloom in the water, and the old legends say the red is her blood still rising. The caves are real. The shrimp are real. Common Questions

What people ask before they drive

Do I need a reservation for Black Sand Beach?

Yes — Wai'ānapanapa State Park has required advance reservations since 2021. Same-day booking is not possible. Book at gostateparks.hawaii.gov up to 30 days ahead. Cars parked outside the park gate will be towed. Download your voucher before leaving Paia as there is no cell signal at the park.

Can I drive the Piilani Highway (Hwy 31) back?

Technically you can drive it in a 4WD vehicle, but many rental agreements restrict portions of it in their agreements. If you take this road and break down or get a flat tire, your roadside assistance is void and you could wait hours for help with no cell signal. We do not recommend it. Drive back the way you came.

How long does the Road to Hana take?

Plan for a full day — 8 to 10 hours. The drive from Kahului to Hana is about 2.5 hours without stops. With the waterfall walks, food stops, Black Sand Beach, and everything else, 8 hours is realistic for seeing it properly. Most people wish they had more time.

Does the app work without cell service?

Yes — this is one of its core design features. Download the tour at your resort or in Paia before you lose signal. Once downloaded, the app uses your phone's GPS (which works without data) to trigger audio stories automatically. You will not need cell service at any point during the drive.

How many people does one purchase cover?

One purchase covers one car — everyone in the vehicle can hear the audio through your car speakers via Bluetooth or aux. You do not need to buy one per person.

Is the Road to Hana worth it if I only have one day?

Yes — but commit to a full day, leave early, and book Black Sand Beach in advance. If you leave after 9 AM without a reservation for Wai'ānapanapa, you'll be arriving at the most crowded times and missing the most photographed stop on the route. Leave early, go properly, and it's one of the best drives in the world.
