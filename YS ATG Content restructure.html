Before You Drive

What you need to know before you enter the park

Which entrance to use — and why it matters

Yellowstone has five entrance gates. Which one you use determines which part of the Grand Loop you experience first and how crowded your first hour inside the park will be. Most visitors enter through the North or West entrances by default — which means Mammoth Hot Springs and Old Faithful are the most congested starting points.

The South Entrance (from Grand Teton) is the least congested on weekday mornings. You arrive at West Thumb Geyser Basin first — a stunning and often overlooked start with Yellowstone Lake behind the thermal features.

When to arrive — the honest guide

Old Faithful's lot fills by 9 AM in July and August. Grand Prismatic Spring is severely crowded 10 AM–4 PM. The Lamar Valley (the best wildlife corridor in North America) is best at dawn and dusk. If you're planning a single day, choose either the geothermal route (Grand Prismatic + Old Faithful) or the wildlife route (Lamar Valley + Hayden Valley) — trying to do both in one day means doing neither well.

Cell service reality: There is no meaningful cell coverage across the vast majority of Yellowstone's interior. Download the tour app at your lodging or in Jackson, Cody, or West Yellowstone before you enter. The tour uses GPS only — no data connection required.

Old Faithful — timing your visit

Old Faithful doesn't erupt on a fixed schedule — it erupts every 44 to 125 minutes depending on the duration of the previous eruption. The NPS Visitor Center displays a prediction board with the next expected eruption time, accurate to within 10 minutes. Arrive at the viewing area 15–20 minutes early. The eruption lasts 1.5–5 minutes and reaches 100–180 feet high.

The side view from the boardwalk loop is less crowded than the main bleacher area and gives you the geyser against the treeline rather than against the Visitor Center. Worth the 5-minute walk.

Wildlife — where to look and when

Lamar Valley in the northeast corner is called "America's Serengeti" — bison herds, wolves, grizzly bears, and pronghorn in the same view. Go at dawn or within an hour of sunset. The audio tour tells you exactly which pullouts to use and which direction to scan. Hayden Valley (central park, near Canyon) is one of the best areas for bison and bears when the connecting park roads are open to regular vehicles.

Bring binoculars. The wildlife is real but often at distance. The audio explains what you're looking at and where to look — most people without a guide don't know which direction to scan from which pullout.

Fuel and food — the practical reality

There are fuel stations inside the park at Old Faithful, Canyon Village, Fishing Bridge, and Grant Village. All accept credit cards. Don't rely on finding an open station after hours — fill up at Canyon or Old Faithful before 7 PM. Dining options inside the park are limited to park lodges (Old Faithful Inn, Lake Yellowstone Hotel, Canyon Lodge) and cafeterias. Stock up in West Yellowstone or Jackson if you're on a budget — park food is expensive and queue times in peak season can exceed an hour.

Key Stops

What the audio tour covers — stop by stop

Stories trigger automatically as you approach each site. You don't navigate — you drive and listen.

Thermal Feature

Grand Prismatic Spring

The largest hot spring in the US and the third largest in the world. The rainbow rings of colour (blue, green, yellow, orange, red) come from heat-resistant microbial mats, each species living at a different temperature. Most visitors see it from the boardwalk — the full aerial view requires the Fairy Falls trail.

The boardwalk can be packed 10 AM–4 PM. Go before 9 AM or after 5 PM. Steam makes photography difficult on cold mornings.
Geyser

Old Faithful

Erupts every 44–125 minutes depending on the prior eruption's length. Reaches 100–180 feet. The prediction board at the Visitor Center gives the next eruption time within 10 minutes. The audio explains the plumbing system below — more complex than most people imagine.

The loop boardwalk offers different angles. The side view (northwest of the main area) is less crowded and worth the short walk.
Wildlife

Lamar Valley

Called "America's Serengeti" — the best place on the continent to see wolves in the wild. Also bison herds, grizzly bears, coyotes, pronghorn, and eagles. Dawn and dusk are the prime windows. The audio identifies the key pullouts by name and tells you which direction to scan.

Bring binoculars. Many visitors miss wolves entirely because they're scanning the wrong hillside. The audio tells you where to look.
Thermal Basin

Norris Geyser Basin

The hottest and most dynamic thermal area in the park. Home to Steamboat Geyser — the world's tallest active geyser (300+ feet), erupting unpredictably. The audio explains why Norris behaves differently from the Upper Geyser Basin and what makes this area geologically unique.

Check the NPS app for recent Steamboat eruption activity — if one has occurred within the past week, the basin will be especially active.
Canyon

Grand Canyon of the Yellowstone

A 20-mile canyon 800–1200 feet deep, carved by the Yellowstone River. Two major waterfalls — Upper Falls (109 ft) and Lower Falls (308 ft). The canyon's yellow walls give the park its name. Artist Point offers the classic postcard view of Lower Falls.

The South Rim trail to Artist Point is 0.4 miles from the parking lot. Do it even if you're short on time — this is one of the most dramatic viewpoints in any national park.
Hot Springs

Mammoth Hot Springs

The only major geothermal area fed by limestone rather than silica. This creates the cascading, terraced travertine formations that look unlike anything else in the park. The features change year by year as water flow shifts. The audio explains what's active and what's dry on the current visit.

Liberty Cap — a 37-foot dormant hot spring cone at the entrance — is one of the most photographed features here and easy to miss if you don't know what you're looking at.
Wildlife

Hayden Valley

The central park's best wildlife viewing after Lamar Valley during the regular road season. Bison herds often cross near the Yellowstone River, and bears may be seen in spring and fall. Birdlife varies by season, so treat wildlife sightings as possibilities, not guarantees. The audio identifies species by behavior, not just appearance.

Traffic jams here are caused by bison — pull over safely and wait. Honking does nothing. The audio has a specific segment on wildlife-jam etiquette that saves a lot of frustration.
Lake

Yellowstone Lake & West Thumb

The largest high-elevation lake in North America, sitting directly on the Yellowstone Caldera. West Thumb Geyser Basin features boiling hot springs erupting on the lake shore. Fishing Bridge spans the Yellowstone River's outlet from the lake — historically one of the best trout fishing spots on earth.

On a clear day the lake reflects the Absaroka Range. The West Thumb boardwalk takes 30–40 minutes and is one of the most surreal walks in the park — boiling water next to a cold alpine lake.
Geyser Basin

Upper Geyser Basin

The highest concentration of geysers on earth — 150 geysers within one square mile. Besides Old Faithful, Beehive, Grand, Castle, and Riverside geysers all erupt on loose schedules. The audio gives you the eruption patterns for each so you can time your walk around the basin.

Spend at least 90 minutes here to have a real chance of seeing multiple geysers erupt. The 3-mile boardwalk loop covers all the major features.

Old Faithful — what "every 90 minutes" actually means

The "erupts every 90 minutes" shorthand is inaccurate. Old Faithful's interval varies from 44 to 125 minutes based on the eruption duration. Longer eruptions = longer interval before the next one.

The NPS posts predictions at the Visitor Center and on the NPS app — accurate to within ±10 minutes. Arrive 15 minutes before the predicted time. Eruption lasts 1.5–5 minutes.

The audio tour explains the geyser's underground plumbing and why it erupts at regular intervals when most geysers don't. Worth understanding before you see it.

After 1.5 min eruption→ Next eruption in ~44–55 min
After 2.5 min eruption→ Next eruption in ~70–85 min
After 3.5+ min eruption→ Next eruption in ~95–125 min

Sample Narration

Hear what plays as you drive

Stories trigger automatically as you approach each site. This is what plays as you arrive at Grand Prismatic Spring.

What you're looking at is life at the extreme edge of what biology permits. The brilliant rings of colour surrounding Grand Prismatic — blue at the centre, through green, yellow, and red at the edges — aren't algae or minerals. They're communities of heat-loving microorganisms called hyperthermophiles. Each ring represents a species living at the precise temperature it can tolerate, from the near-boiling 190°F at the centre, where only chemosynthetic bacteria survive, to the cooler 130°F at the outer edges, where photosynthetic organisms can function. The spring is 370 feet across and 160 feet deep — you could fit a football field inside it. And the entire thing is sitting directly above a magma chamber that has erupted catastrophically three times in the past two million years. You're standing on a supervolcano right now.

Common Questions

What people ask before they drive into the park

Does Yellowstone require timed entry permits?

No general timed-entry or vehicle reservation is required for standard Yellowstone entry. You do need a park entrance pass. Some activities — including lodging, camping, fishing, boating, backcountry camping, research, commercial use, filming, weddings, and special events — may require separate reservations, permits, or authorizations. Always check the official NPS Yellowstone pages before travel.

When is the best time to visit Yellowstone?

Early June and September are the sweet spots — lower crowds, wildlife still active, most facilities open. July–August is peak season with significant congestion at Old Faithful and Grand Prismatic. May can have road closures. Winter (Dec–Feb) is spectacular but requires snowcoach or snowmobile access.

Can you do Yellowstone in one day?

You can see highlights in one day but you can't do it properly. The Grand Loop is 142 miles — driving it all plus stopping takes 3 full days minimum. For one day, choose either the southern loop (Old Faithful + Grand Prismatic + Yellowstone Lake) or the northern loop (Mammoth + Norris + Grand Canyon). Not both.

Does the app work inside the park without cell service?

Yes — completely. Download the tour before entering the park. The app uses your phone's built-in GPS (which doesn't require data) to trigger stories automatically as you drive. You will have essentially no cell service inside Yellowstone and won't need any once the tour is downloaded.

Is the $35 park entrance fee included?

No — the $35 vehicle fee (valid 7 days for everyone in your car) is paid at the park entrance gate, separate from the $9.99 tour. If you're visiting multiple national parks in one year, the America the Beautiful Annual Pass at $80 covers all of them and is worth it at two park visits.

How close can you get to the geysers and wildlife?

Geysers and thermal features: stay on boardwalks at all times — the ground looks solid but the crust above thermal features is fragile and superheated water is immediately below. Wildlife: NPS rules require staying 25 yards from bison and elk, 100 yards from bears and wolves. Breaking these rules results in fines — the distances exist because people have been seriously injured ignoring them.
