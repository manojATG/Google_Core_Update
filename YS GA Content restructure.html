How to structure your visit — 1, 2 or 3 days

Yellowstone is 142 miles around the Grand Loop — too large to rush. Here's how to plan depending on how long you have. The GuideAlong app includes these itineraries built in.

1 day
Southern Loop — Geothermal Focus

Best for: First-timers who want the iconic Yellowstone

Enter the West Entrance → Madison Junction → Old Faithful (check eruption prediction on arrival → arrive at viewing area 15 min early) → Grand Prismatic Spring → West Thumb Geyser Basin → exit South Entrance when open, or return West. Drive time without stops: ~3 hours. Full day with stops: 8–10 hours. Leave your resort or hotel no later than 7 AM.

Grand Prismatic Spring

Thermal

Largest hot spring in the US (370 ft across). The rainbow rings of colour are microbial mats — each species tolerates a different temperature, creating the visible spectrum from centre to edge.

Best timeBefore 9 AM or after 5 PM
Crowd levelHigh 10 AM–4 PM
BoardwalkYes — 0.5 mi loop
Aerial viewFairy Falls trail, 1.6 mi RT
The steam makes photography difficult on cold mornings. Go on a warm, dry day for the best colour saturation.

Old Faithful Geyser

Geyser

Erupts every 44–125 minutes (not "every 90 minutes" — this is a common misconception). Eruption duration determines the next interval. Height ranges 100–180 feet. Duration: 1.5–5 minutes.

Grand Canyon of the Yellowstone

Canyon

20-mile canyon, 800–1,200 ft deep, carved by the Yellowstone River. Two major waterfalls: Upper Falls (109 ft) and Lower Falls (308 ft, twice the height of Niagara). The yellow canyon walls give the park its name.

Best viewpointArtist Point (South Rim)
Walk to Artist Point0.4 mi from parking
Lower Falls height308 feet
North Rim trailMore dramatic angles
Do not skip Artist Point even if you're short on time. The view of the Lower Falls against the yellow canyon walls is one of the most dramatic in any national park.

Norris Geyser Basin

Thermal

Hottest and most dynamic thermal area in the park. Home to Steamboat Geyser — world's tallest active geyser at 300+ feet, erupting unpredictably. The basin changes significantly year to year.

Steamboat eruptionsCheck NPS current conditions
Boardwalk2 loops, ~2 miles total
TemperatureHottest ground in the park
Time needed1.5–2 hours

Mammoth Hot Springs

Thermal

The only major geothermal area fed by limestone. Creates terraced travertine formations unlike anything else in the park. Active features change year by year as water flow shifts between terraces.

When to Visit

Yellowstone by season — what each month actually means

April — May

Shoulder Season

Roads opening progressively — Beartooth Highway typically opens late May. Wildlife very active (newborn bison calves, active bears). Some facilities closed. Weather unpredictable — snow possible any month.

⭐⭐⭐⭐ Wildlife excellent / some road closures
June — August

Peak Season

All roads and facilities open. Warmest weather. Maximum congestion — Old Faithful lot fills by 9 AM in July. Wildlife less visible mid-day (avoiding heat). Book lodging 6+ months ahead.

⭐⭐⭐ Great access / maximum crowds
September — October

Best Overall

Crowds drop sharply after Labor Day. Wildlife extremely active — fall elk rut (bugling) begins in September. Aspen trees turn gold in October. Most facilities still open. Cooler temperatures keep wildlife more visible all day.

⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ Best combination of access, crowds, wildlife
December — March

Winter

The North Entrance to Northeast Entrance corridor via Mammoth, Tower Junction, and Cooke City is generally open year-round to regular vehicles, conditions permitting. Most interior park roads close to regular vehicles in winter and shift to limited oversnow travel by snowcoach or snowmobile. Geothermal features can be dramatic in cold weather, but winter access requires careful NPS road-status checks.

⭐⭐⭐⭐ Spectacular / requires winter planning

Lamar Valley · Sample narration

The wolves came back

For 70 years, there were no wolves in Yellowstone. They'd been systematically killed off by the 1920s — the government's position at the time was that wolves were a threat to livestock and ungulate populations. Without them, the elk herds grew enormous, overgrazed the riverbanks, and the willows and aspens that stabilize stream banks disappeared. The rivers themselves began to change shape. In 1995, 14 gray wolves were brought from Canada and released here in Lamar Valley. What happened next became one of ecology's most famous case studies: the wolves changed not just the elk behavior but the rivers themselves. The elk avoided lingering in open valleys and riverbanks. The vegetation recovered. The roots held the soil. The rivers narrowed and deepened. The effect of one predator on a landscape — right here, in the valley you're looking at right now.

Common Questions

What people ask before their Yellowstone trip

Does Yellowstone require timed entry reservations?

No general timed-entry or vehicle reservation is required for standard Yellowstone entry. A park entrance pass is required, and some activities — lodging, camping, fishing, boating, backcountry camping, research, commercial use, filming, weddings, and special events — may require separate reservations, permits, or authorizations. Check the official NPS Yellowstone pages before travel because policies and conditions can change.

Is the park entry fee ($35) included in the tour price?

No — the $35/vehicle entry fee (valid 7 days) is paid at the park entrance gate, separate from the $19.99 GuideAlong tour. If you're visiting 2+ national parks in a year, the America the Beautiful Annual Pass ($80) covers all national parks and federal lands and quickly pays for itself.

Does the GuideAlong app work without cell service in the park?

Yes — fully offline once downloaded. Download the tour at your hotel or lodging before you drive to the park. The app uses your phone's built-in GPS (which works without data) to trigger stories automatically. There is essentially no cell signal in most of Yellowstone's interior — plan accordingly.

How many days do I need for Yellowstone?

Minimum 2 days to see the park properly. 3 days is ideal — one day for the southern geothermal loop (Old Faithful + Grand Prismatic), one for the northern wildlife loop (Lamar Valley + Mammoth + Canyon), and a third day to revisit your favourites without rushing. The app includes suggested 1-day, 2-day, and 3-day itineraries built in.

What's the difference between the single park tour and the Yellowstone + Grand Teton combo?

The single tour covers Yellowstone only — all 5 entrances, the full Grand Loop, and walking tours of Old Faithful, Grand Prismatic, and West Thumb. The Yellowstone + Grand Teton combo adds full coverage of Grand Teton National Park, the John D. Rockefeller Parkway, and Jackson Hole. Most visitors to Yellowstone pass through or visit Grand Teton — the combo is usually the better value.

When do the walking tours of Old Faithful and Grand Prismatic start?

The walking tours begin automatically when your GPS detects you've parked at or near the trailhead. You'll see a prompt in the app to switch from the driving tour to the walking tour. Both walking tours are included in the standard Yellowstone tour — no separate purchase needed.
